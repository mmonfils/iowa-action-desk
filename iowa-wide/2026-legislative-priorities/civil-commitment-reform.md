# Action Alert: Civil Commitment Law

### 1. Policy Briefing
*   **Situation:** Current state commitment codes block early medical intervention, forcing families to wait until an individual presents an immediate danger.
*   **Background:** Iowa legal codes under [Iowa Code Chapter 229](https://www.legis.iowa.gov) set an incredibly high threshold for involuntary assistance, meaning interventions occur only during acute police encounters.
*   **Assessment:** This dynamic delays vital care until an avoidable emergency takes place. Systems theory shows that this statutory barrier shifts the burden of psychiatric crises directly onto local police and county jail cells rather than processing them through medical environments.
*   **Recommendation:** Amend state commitment laws to establish safe avenues for early, less restrictive outpatient clinical assistance.
