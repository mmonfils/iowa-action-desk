# Action Alert: Psychiatric Bed Capacity

### 1. Policy Briefing
*   **Situation:** Iowa faces a deep deficit in inpatient beds for adults and youth. This structural bottleneck leaves residents languishing in local emergency departments for days without treatment.
*   **Background:** Legislative funding choices have shrunk public medical beds over the last decade. Active bills like [Iowa House File 2292](https://www.legis.iowa.gov/legislation/BillBook?ga=91&ba=HF2292) mandate doubling public bed counts to clear out these backlogs.
*   **Assessment:** According to the [Iowa Legislative Services Agency Fiscal Report](https://www.legis.iowa.gov/docs/publications/FN/1601896.pdf), fixing this gap requires substantial ongoing state appropriations. From a systems perspective, when the state fails to fund hospital care, local emergency rooms and local jail networks are forced to bear the cost.
*   **Recommendation:** Pass and fund the provisions within House File 2292 to double state hospital beds by 2027 and build a resilient health network.

### 3. Sample Script
Connect the request to your personal observations of community stress before stating the policy demand.

> "Hello, my name is a constituent living in your area. I am calling because I see how our community feels the strain when public resources are extracted from our neighborhoods. I watch families struggle for days in local emergency rooms because Iowa lacks immediate psychiatric hospital beds. We need a system that actively invests in neighborhood stability rather than creating structural friction for regular families. I urge you to support House File 2292 to double inpatient bed capacity at our state mental health institutes. Will you commit to voting in favor of funding this public measure?"
