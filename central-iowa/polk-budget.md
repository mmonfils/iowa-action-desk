# Action Alert: Polk County Budget

### 1. Policy Briefing
*   **Situation:** Local funding for mobile crisis response teams and neighborhood drop-in wellness centers faces potential operational pressures in the upcoming county fiscal cycles.
*   **Background:** The Polk County Board of Supervisors manages regional discretionary local funds. According to the [Polk County Board of Supervisors Budget Resolution](https://www.polkcountyiowa.gov/media/pvuk1jd0/budget-fy25-26-budget-amendment-5-19-26-pdf), millions of dollars flow through complex fund transfers and department appropriations each fiscal year.
*   **Assessment:** Reviewing formal [Polk County Budget Presentation data logs](https://www.polkcountyiowa.gov/board-of-supervisors/2026-budget-information/) reveals that behavioral health and disability services require robust, ongoing local financial support to remain effective. Systems theory emphasizes that defunding mobile crisis teams shifts the burden onto municipal police departments and emergency rooms, creating substantial structural friction across the local community environment.
*   **Recommendation:** Protect and expand county appropriations for community-based mobile crisis response teams and behavioral health services in the ongoing budget cycles.

### 3. Sample Script
Connect the request to your personal observations of community stress before stating the policy demand.

> "Hello, my name is a resident of Polk County. I am calling because I see how our community feels the strain when public resources are extracted from our neighborhoods. Our local mobile crisis teams keep people safe and out of jail during health emergencies. We need a system that actively invests in neighborhood stability rather than creating structural friction for regular families. I urge the Board to fully fund mobile crisis units and mental health hubs in this budget cycle. Will you commit to protecting these public services?"
