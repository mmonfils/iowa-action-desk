# Ward 4 Housing Stability and Code Enforcement

### Analytical Context
Recent municipal tracking reveals an intentional shift toward prolonged inspection cycles for multi-family rental properties across Southeast Des Moines. This administrative delay increases the structural friction borne by low-income tenants, who must navigate substandard living conditions while corporate landlords defer basic maintenance. By tracking public civil citation records via Open Data Des Moines, we find that enforcement backlogs disproportionately affect high-density working-class tracts.

> **Analytical Framework:** This stance is informed by a systems-theory perspective on local resource distribution and verified via Open Data Des Moines rental inspection history logs.
